Autonomous Quadruped

CatBot

A lightweight, highly agile autonomous quadruped reconnaissance platform — stealthy locomotion, navigation through constrained and unstructured environments, and real-time situational awareness without continuous human supervision.

Ongoing Bio-mimetic Jetson + MCU ~2.2 kg

Objective

Design and develop a lightweight, highly agile autonomous quadruped robotic platform capable of stealthy locomotion, navigation in constrained and unstructured environments, and real-time situational awareness — operating without continuous human supervision.

System overview

CatBot is a bio-mimetic quadruped inspired by large feline biomechanics, emphasising superior locomotion, low acoustic output, dynamic balance and rapid directional control. The platform integrates vision-based perception, inertial sensing and closed-loop motor control to enable terrain-aware locomotion and autonomous navigation.

High-level perception and planning run on an onboard NVIDIA Jetson-class compute module, while real-time control and sensor acquisition are delegated to embedded microcontrollers. The result is a compact, self-contained unit with a target gross mass of approximately 2.2 kg.

Technical specifications

Processor
NVIDIA Jetson Nano Super (high-level perception) · STM32 / ESP32-class MCUs (real-time control & I/O)
Sensors
StereoLabs ZED X Mini stereo vision module, inertial sensing (IMU)
Actuation
Closed-loop motor control with feedback for dynamic balance & directional control
Architecture
Distributed compute — perception/planning on Jetson, real-time loops on MCUs
Target mass
≈ 2.2 kg, compact self-contained unit

Engineering emphasis

Bio-mimetic locomotion

Feline-inspired biomechanics drive a gait optimised for agility, dynamic balance and low acoustic output rather than raw payload.

Split compute architecture

Heavy perception and planning sit on a Jetson; hard real-time control and sensor acquisition are isolated on dedicated MCUs for deterministic timing.

Terrain-aware autonomy

Stereo vision and inertial sensing fuse into closed-loop control so the platform stays balanced and oriented across unstructured terrain.
